# Grade 6 Social Studies: Map Skills

Subject: Social Studies
Grade: 6
Unit: Geography
Lesson: Map Skills
Estimated Time: 45 minutes

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should recognize basic map symbols and understand cardinal directions.

## Learning Objectives

Students will interpret physical and political maps, use map scales, and analyze geographic information to answer questions about location and movement.

## Standards

Students use geographic tools and representations to analyze relationships between people, places, and environments.

## Materials

- Political map
- Physical map
- Atlas
- Rulers
- Student notebook

## Lesson Overview

Students practice reading different types of maps and using map elements such as legends, scales, and coordinates to solve geographic problems.

## Teacher Instructions

Review map elements including title, legend, compass rose, and scale. Demonstrate how different maps communicate different kinds of geographic information.

## Guided Practice

Students locate cities, rivers, and mountain ranges using maps and answer questions using the map legend and scale.

## Independent Practice

Students complete a worksheet interpreting several maps and explaining how geography influences human activity.

## Differentiation

Provide simplified maps for students needing additional support. Challenge advanced students to compare thematic maps with political maps.

## Assessment

Students complete an exit ticket identifying map elements and explaining how a scale helps estimate distance.

## Vocabulary

- Scale
- Legend
- Compass Rose
- Latitude
- Longitude
- Physical Map
- Political Map

## Extensions

Students create a simple map of their neighborhood using an appropriate legend and scale.
