# Grade 4 Mathematics: Comparing Fractions

Subject: Mathematics  
Grade: 4  
Unit: Fractions  
Lesson: Comparing Fractions  
Estimated Time: 45 minutes  

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should understand numerator, denominator, equal parts, and basic fraction models.

## Learning Objectives

Students will compare two fractions with different numerators or denominators using visual models, number lines, and reasoning about fraction size.

## Standards

Students compare fractions by reasoning about their size and using visual fraction models.

## Materials

- Fraction strips
- Number line handouts
- Colored pencils
- Whiteboard
- Student comparison cards

## Lesson Overview

This lesson helps students compare fractions by using visual models and number lines before introducing symbolic comparison.

## Teacher Instructions

Begin by showing 1/2 and 1/4 using fraction strips. Ask students which fraction is greater and why. Emphasize that a larger denominator does not always mean a larger fraction.

## Guided Practice

Students compare 2/3 and 3/6 using visual models. Then they place both fractions on a number line and explain their reasoning.

## Independent Practice

Students complete fraction comparison problems and write short explanations using words, models, or number lines.

## Differentiation

Provide pre-labeled models for students needing support. Challenge advanced students to compare fractions with unlike denominators and justify their reasoning.

## Assessment

Students complete an exit ticket comparing 3/4 and 2/3, explaining which is greater and why.

## Vocabulary

- Compare
- Greater than
- Less than
- Equivalent
- Numerator
- Denominator

## Extensions

Students create their own fraction comparison problem and explain the solution using a visual model.
