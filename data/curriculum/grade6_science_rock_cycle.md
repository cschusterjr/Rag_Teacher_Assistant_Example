# Grade 6 Science: The Rock Cycle

Subject: Science
Grade: 6
Unit: Earth Science
Lesson: The Rock Cycle
Estimated Time: 50 minutes

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should understand that Earth materials can change over time through natural processes and be familiar with basic types of rocks.

## Learning Objectives

Students will describe the three major rock types and explain how heat, pressure, weathering, erosion, melting, and cooling drive the rock cycle.

## Standards

Students develop models describing the cycling of Earth's materials through geologic processes.

## Materials

- Rock samples
- Rock cycle diagram
- Magnifying glass
- Student science notebook
- Colored pencils

## Lesson Overview

Students investigate how igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks form and transform over time. Through diagrams and real rock samples, students explore how Earth's processes continuously recycle materials.

## Teacher Instructions

Introduce the three major rock types using physical rock samples. Discuss how each rock forms before presenting the complete rock cycle diagram. Emphasize that the rock cycle is continuous rather than linear.

## Guided Practice

Students complete a class rock cycle diagram by identifying the processes that transform one rock type into another.

## Independent Practice

Students label a blank rock cycle diagram and explain each transformation process using complete sentences.

## Differentiation

Provide partially completed diagrams for students needing support. Challenge advanced students to explain how tectonic activity influences the rock cycle.

## Assessment

Students complete an exit ticket identifying the processes responsible for forming each rock type and explaining one complete pathway through the rock cycle.

## Vocabulary

- Igneous
- Sedimentary
- Metamorphic
- Weathering
- Erosion
- Melting
- Cooling

## Extensions

Students research a famous geological location and explain how the rock cycle contributed to its formation.
