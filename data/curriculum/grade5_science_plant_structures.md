# Grade 5 Science: Plant Structures

Subject: Science
Grade: 5
Unit: Life Science
Lesson: Plant Structures
Estimated Time: 50 minutes

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should understand that living things have basic needs and be able to identify common plants and their parts.

## Learning Objectives

Students will identify the major structures of flowering plants and explain how each structure contributes to the plant's survival.

## Standards

Students develop models describing how plant structures support growth, reproduction, and survival.

## Materials

- Live flowering plant
- Plant diagrams
- Hand lens
- Science notebook
- Colored pencils

## Lesson Overview

Students investigate the function of roots, stems, leaves, flowers, fruits, and seeds. They observe real plants and connect each structure to its role in helping the plant survive.

## Teacher Instructions

Display a live flowering plant and ask students to identify as many parts as they can. Introduce each structure one at a time, discussing its purpose before having students examine the plant more closely.

## Guided Practice

Students label a plant diagram together and discuss how each structure helps the plant obtain water, sunlight, nutrients, or reproduce.

## Independent Practice

Students draw and label a flowering plant, then write one sentence describing the function of each major structure.

## Differentiation

Provide labeled diagrams for students who need additional support. Challenge advanced students to compare flowering and non-flowering plants.

## Assessment

Students complete an exit ticket matching plant structures with their functions and explain why leaves are essential for plant survival.

## Vocabulary

- Root
- Stem
- Leaf
- Flower
- Fruit
- Seed
- Photosynthesis

## Extensions

Students observe plants outdoors or at home and record examples of how different plant structures vary across species.
