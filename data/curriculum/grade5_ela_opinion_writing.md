# Grade 5 English Language Arts: Opinion Writing

Subject: English Language Arts
Grade: 5
Unit: Writing
Lesson: Opinion Writing
Estimated Time: 55 minutes

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should be able to write complete paragraphs and identify supporting details.

## Learning Objectives

Students will write an organized opinion paragraph that includes a clear claim, supporting reasons, evidence, and a concluding statement.

## Standards

Students write opinion pieces supporting a point of view with logically ordered reasons and relevant evidence.

## Materials

- Graphic organizer
- Writing notebook
- Sample opinion essay
- Highlighters
- Peer review checklist

## Lesson Overview

Students learn how to organize opinion writing by developing a clear claim, supporting it with evidence, and concluding effectively.

## Teacher Instructions

Model how to brainstorm ideas, organize supporting reasons, and draft an opinion paragraph. Emphasize using evidence rather than personal preference alone.

## Guided Practice

As a class, develop an opinion paragraph on a familiar school topic using a graphic organizer.

## Independent Practice

Students write their own opinion paragraph, revise it using peer feedback, and submit a final draft.

## Differentiation

Provide sentence frames for students needing support. Challenge advanced students to include counterarguments and rebuttals.

## Assessment

Students complete a writing rubric demonstrating a clear claim, supporting evidence, organization, and conventions.

## Vocabulary

- Opinion
- Claim
- Evidence
- Reason
- Counterargument
- Conclusion

## Extensions

Students write an opinion letter to the principal proposing an improvement to the school and support their position with evidence.
