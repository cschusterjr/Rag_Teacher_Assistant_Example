# Grade 5 ELA: Identifying Main Idea

Subject: English Language Arts  
Grade: 5  
Unit: Reading Informational Text  
Lesson: Identifying Main Idea  
Estimated Time: 50 minutes  

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should be able to identify topic, details, and basic text features in informational passages.

## Learning Objectives

Students will identify the main idea of an informational text and explain how key details support it.

## Standards

Students determine main ideas and summarize informational texts using supporting details.

## Materials

- Informational passage
- Highlighters
- Main idea graphic organizer
- Projector or chart paper

## Lesson Overview

This lesson teaches students to distinguish between a broad topic and a specific main idea. Students practice identifying key details that support the author's central point.

## Teacher Instructions

Display a short informational paragraph. Ask students to identify the topic first, then guide them to state what the author wants readers to understand about that topic.

## Guided Practice

Read a passage together. Students highlight three important details and use them to write one main idea statement.

## Independent Practice

Students read a new passage independently and complete a graphic organizer with topic, main idea, and supporting details.

## Differentiation

Provide sentence frames for students who need support. Give advanced students a passage with multiple related main ideas.

## Assessment

Students write a one-sentence main idea and list two supporting details from the passage.

## Vocabulary

- Main idea
- Topic
- Supporting detail
- Summary
- Informational text

## Extensions

Students compare the main ideas of two short passages on the same topic.
