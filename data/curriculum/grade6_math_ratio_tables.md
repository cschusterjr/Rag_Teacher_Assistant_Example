# Grade 6 Mathematics: Ratio Tables

Subject: Mathematics
Grade: 6
Unit: Ratios
Lesson: Ratio Tables
Estimated Time: 50 minutes

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should understand multiplication, division, equivalent fractions, and basic proportional reasoning.

## Learning Objectives

Students will use ratio tables to represent equivalent ratios and solve real-world problems involving proportional relationships.

## Standards

Students use ratio reasoning to solve problems by creating and interpreting ratio tables.

## Materials

- Ratio table worksheet
- Whiteboard
- Calculators (optional)
- Colored pencils
- Student notebooks

## Lesson Overview

Students learn how ratio tables organize equivalent ratios and help solve comparison problems. The lesson emphasizes identifying multiplicative relationships rather than additive patterns.

## Teacher Instructions

Begin with a simple ratio, such as 2 apples for every 3 oranges. Demonstrate how multiplying both quantities by the same number produces equivalent ratios. Record each equivalent ratio in a ratio table.

Discuss how ratio tables help identify patterns and solve missing-value problems.

## Guided Practice

Students complete partially filled ratio tables together, explaining how they determined each missing value.

## Independent Practice

Students solve real-world problems involving recipes, classroom supplies, and map scales by constructing ratio tables.

## Differentiation

Provide completed first rows and multiplication charts for students needing additional support. Challenge advanced students to compare ratio tables with coordinate graphs representing proportional relationships.

## Assessment

Students complete an exit ticket by filling in a ratio table and explaining why all rows represent equivalent ratios.

## Vocabulary

- Ratio
- Equivalent Ratio
- Ratio Table
- Proportion
- Multiplicative Relationship

## Extensions

Students design their own real-world ratio problem and solve it using a ratio table.
