# Grade 6 English Language Arts: Theme vs. Main Idea

Subject: English Language Arts
Grade: 6
Unit: Literature
Lesson: Theme vs. Main Idea
Estimated Time: 50 minutes

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should be able to summarize a text, identify key details, and recognize the main idea in informational passages.

## Learning Objectives

Students will distinguish between the theme of a literary work and the main idea of an informational text. Students will support their thinking with evidence from the text.

## Standards

Students determine the theme or central idea of a text and explain how it develops through details and events.

## Materials

- Short story
- Informational article
- Graphic organizer
- Highlighters
- Student notebook

## Lesson Overview

Students compare literary and informational texts to understand the difference between theme and main idea. They practice supporting their conclusions with textual evidence.

## Teacher Instructions

Begin by reviewing the definition of main idea using an informational article. Then introduce theme using a short story. Discuss how theme communicates a broader message while the main idea explains what a text is mostly about.

## Guided Practice

Read a short story together and identify possible themes. Then read an informational article and determine its main idea using supporting details.

## Independent Practice

Students read two passages independently and complete a comparison chart identifying the theme of the literary text and the main idea of the informational text.

## Differentiation

Provide sentence starters and graphic organizers for students needing support. Challenge advanced students to identify multiple themes within a single story and justify their reasoning.

## Assessment

Students complete an exit ticket explaining the difference between theme and main idea and provide one example of each.

## Vocabulary

- Theme
- Main Idea
- Supporting Detail
- Text Evidence
- Literary Text
- Informational Text

## Extensions

Students compare two stories with similar themes and explain how different authors communicate the same message.
