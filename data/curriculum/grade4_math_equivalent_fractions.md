# Grade 4 Mathematics: Equivalent Fractions

Subject: Mathematics  
Grade: 4  
Unit: Fractions  
Lesson: Equivalent Fractions  
Estimated Time: 45 minutes  

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should be able to identify numerator and denominator, recognize simple fractions such as 1/2, 1/3, and 1/4, and understand that fractions represent equal parts of a whole.

## Learning Objectives

Students will explain why two fractions are equivalent using visual models. Students will generate equivalent fractions by partitioning fraction models into equal parts.

## Standards

Students develop understanding of fraction equivalence and ordering by using visual fraction models and reasoning about the size of fractions.

## Materials

- Fraction strips
- Blank fraction bars
- Colored pencils
- Whiteboard or chart paper
- Student practice handout

## Lesson Overview

This lesson introduces equivalent fractions through visual models. Students compare fractions such as 1/2 and 2/4 by using fraction strips and shaded fraction bars. The emphasis is on conceptual understanding before using numerical procedures.

## Teacher Instructions

Begin by showing one fraction strip divided into two equal parts and another divided into four equal parts. Shade 1/2 on the first strip and 2/4 on the second strip. Ask students what they notice about the shaded portions.

Guide students to explain that the fractions are equivalent because they cover the same amount of the whole, even though the number of parts is different.

## Guided Practice

Have students use blank fraction bars to model 1/3 and 2/6. Ask students to compare the shaded regions and explain whether the fractions are equivalent.

Then model 3/4 and 6/8. Ask students to describe how the number of equal parts changed while the total shaded amount stayed the same.

## Independent Practice

Students complete problems where they shade models to show equivalent fractions. They also explain in writing why two given fractions are equivalent or not equivalent.

## Differentiation

For students who need support, provide pre-divided fraction strips and focus on visual matching. For students who are ready for extension, ask them to generate multiple equivalent fractions for the same amount.

## Assessment

Use an exit ticket with three tasks: identify whether 1/2 and 3/6 are equivalent, draw a model for 2/3 and 4/6, and explain why equivalent fractions represent the same amount.

## Vocabulary

- Fraction
- Numerator
- Denominator
- Equivalent
- Whole
- Equal parts

## Extensions

Students create a visual poster showing at least three fractions equivalent to 1/2 and explain their reasoning using words and models.
