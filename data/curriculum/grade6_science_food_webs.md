# Grade 6 Science: Food Webs

Subject: Science  
Grade: 6  
Unit: Ecosystems  
Lesson: Food Webs  
Estimated Time: 50 minutes  

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should understand producers, consumers, decomposers, and basic food chains.

## Learning Objectives

Students will explain how energy moves through an ecosystem and construct a food web showing relationships among organisms.

## Standards

Students model the flow of energy and matter in ecosystems.

## Materials

- Ecosystem organism cards
- Chart paper
- Markers
- Food web diagram example
- Student notebook

## Lesson Overview

This lesson expands student understanding from simple food chains to interconnected food webs. Students analyze how organisms depend on one another for energy.

## Teacher Instructions

Review a simple food chain such as grass, rabbit, and fox. Then ask students what might happen if another predator or food source is added.

## Guided Practice

As a class, build a food web using organism cards. Draw arrows to show the direction of energy flow.

## Independent Practice

Students create their own food web for a forest, ocean, or desert ecosystem and explain at least three relationships.

## Differentiation

Provide partially completed food webs for students needing support. Ask advanced students to predict the effect of removing one organism.

## Assessment

Students explain how energy moves from the sun to producers and then to consumers in a food web.

## Vocabulary

- Producer
- Consumer
- Decomposer
- Ecosystem
- Food web
- Energy flow

## Extensions

Students write a short explanation predicting how a food web would change if one organism population decreased.
