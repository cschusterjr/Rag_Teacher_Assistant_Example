# Grade 5 Social Studies: The American Revolution

Subject: Social Studies
Grade: 5
Unit: United States History
Lesson: The American Revolution
Estimated Time: 50 minutes

## Prerequisite Skills

Students should understand the concept of colonies and have basic knowledge of British rule in North America.

## Learning Objectives

Students will explain the causes of the American Revolution, identify important events, and describe how the conflict led to the founding of the United States.

## Standards

Students analyze the causes and major events of the American Revolution and explain its significance in American history.

## Materials

- Timeline cards
- Colonial America map
- Primary source excerpts
- Student notebook
- Chart paper

## Lesson Overview

Students investigate the growing tensions between Great Britain and the American colonies. They explore key events leading to the Revolutionary War and examine how the conflict changed the course of American history.

## Teacher Instructions

Begin by discussing taxation without representation and introduce major events such as the Boston Tea Party and the Battles of Lexington and Concord. Encourage students to consider multiple perspectives.

## Guided Practice

Students place important Revolutionary events in chronological order and discuss how each contributed to the outbreak of war.

## Independent Practice

Students create a timeline illustrating major Revolutionary events and write a paragraph explaining which event they believe was most significant.

## Differentiation

Provide visual timelines and vocabulary support for students needing assistance. Challenge advanced students to analyze excerpts from the Declaration of Independence.

## Assessment

Students complete an exit ticket identifying two causes of the American Revolution and one important outcome.

## Vocabulary

- Colony
- Patriot
- Loyalist
- Revolution
- Independence
- Parliament

## Extensions

Students compare the American Revolution with another historical independence movement.
